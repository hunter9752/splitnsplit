Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about joining or hosting a split

How do payments work?

When you join a split, you'll pay your share directly through our secure platform. For host-created splits, the payment goes into an escrow account until the group is formed, after which it's released to the service provider. For existing subscription splits, the payment goes to the host who manages the subscription.

What happens if a member drops out?

If a member drops out, the host can find a replacement member to keep the cost distribution the same. If no replacement is found, the remaining members may need to cover the additional cost until a replacement is found. Each group sets its own policy on this, so check the group details before joining.

Will my personal information be shared?

We value your privacy. Only the information necessary for managing the split will be shared with group members. For example, the host might need your email to add you to a subscription service, but your payment details and other personal information remain private.

Can I leave a split before the subscription ends?

Yes, you can leave a split, but there may be terms set by the group host. Typically, you'll need to give advance notice (usually 30 days) and might need to help find a replacement. Some groups may have cancellation fees for early exits, so check the group's policy before joining.

How do I ensure everyone pays their fair share?

Our platform includes automated payment reminders and tracking. As a host, you can set up automatic collection of payments for the duration of the subscription. Members who consistently miss payments can be removed from the group according to the terms set by the host.
